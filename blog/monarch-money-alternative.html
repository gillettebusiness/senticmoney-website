Why are people looking for Monarch Money alternatives?

People are searching for Monarch Money alternatives because of its $99.99/year (annual) or $14.99/month (~$180/year on monthly billing) price tag, its dependence on Plaid for bank connections, and persistent concerns about storing sensitive financial data in the cloud.

The story behind this search trend starts with Mint. When Intuit shut down Mint in January 2024 and migrated users to Credit Karma, millions of budget-conscious users needed a replacement fast. Monarch Money was widely positioned as the natural successor — it had a similar feature set, a polished interface, and even ran promotional pricing to attract Mint refugees. Many users made the switch. Now, a year later, a growing number of those same users are looking to move again.

Here are the main reasons people leave Monarch Money:

  • Price shock: At $99.99/year (annual) or $14.99/month, Monarch is one of the most expensive budgeting apps on the market.
  • Plaid dependency: Monarch relies on Plaid and Finicity to connect to your bank accounts. Our article on whether Plaid is safe covers the security trade-offs in detail.
  • Cloud storage concerns: Monarch stores all your financial data on its servers. For users who have received data breach notifications or simply prefer to keep their finances private, this is a dealbreaker.
  • Feature mismatch: Monarch is built around passive, automatic tracking. Users who prefer zero-based budgeting, envelope methods, or paycheck-to-paycheck planning find Monarch's tools a poor fit.
  • Offline access: Monarch requires an active internet connection. Users who want their budget available offline need an alternative.

Best Monarch Money alternatives in 2026

Beyond SenticMoney, several other apps are worth considering depending on your budgeting philosophy and comfort level with cloud storage and Plaid connections.

YNAB (You Need A Budget)

YNAB costs $109/year (annual) or $14.99/month and is built entirely around zero-based budgeting — every dollar gets a job before you spend it. It has an exceptionally strong methodology and a dedicated community. The trade-offs: cloud-based, Plaid-connected, and at $109/year it is actually more expensive than Monarch. Best for users who specifically want guided zero-based budgeting.

EveryDollar

Dave Ramsey's budgeting app uses zero-based budgeting. The free tier supports manual entry; the paid tier ($17.99/month or ~$79.99/year via Ramsey+) adds Plaid-based bank sync. Cheaper than Monarch at the annual price but still cloud-based and Plaid-dependent on the paid tier. A solid option for Ramsey followers.

GoodBudget

GoodBudget uses the envelope budgeting method and offers a free tier (10 envelopes) and a paid tier at $80/year or $8/month. Unlike most competitors, GoodBudget does not connect to banks at all — even on the paid tier, all entry is manual. No Plaid exposure, but more manual work. A reasonable choice for envelope-method purists.

Quicken Simplifi

Quicken Simplifi costs $71.88/year (annual only, no monthly option) and offers strong reports. Cloud-based, Plaid-connected, no free tier. A good fit for users who want solid reporting and are comfortable with Plaid, but it does not offer a fundamentally different privacy story from Monarch.

How to switch from Monarch Money to SenticMoney

Switching from Monarch Money to SenticMoney takes about 15–20 minutes: export your transaction history from Monarch as a CSV file, import it into SenticMoney, set up your categories, and configure your budget.

  1. Export your transactions from Monarch Money. In Monarch, go to Settings and look for the Export Data option. Download your transactions as a CSV file.
  2. Download SenticMoney. Visit senticmoney.com/download and install the Windows or Mac application. The free tier is available immediately — no account required.
  3. Import your Monarch CSV. In SenticMoney, go to Transactions, click Import, and select your exported Monarch CSV file. SenticMoney supports CSV, Excel, OFX, QFX, and PDF imports, plus 15+ bank-specific presets (Chase, BofA, Wells Fargo, Citi, Capital One, and more).
  4. Set up your categories. Match your SenticMoney categories to the ones you used in Monarch. Custom categories, subcategories, colors, and icons let you replicate your existing structure.
  5. Configure your budget. Choose your method — zero-based, 50/30/20, pay-yourself-first, envelope, or Runway for paycheck-to-paycheck planning.
  6. Set up recurring bills. Add your bills and subscriptions manually in the Bills section so SenticMoney can track due dates and show them on your Financial Calendar.

The main adjustment is moving from automatic Plaid syncing to manual CSV imports. Most users find this takes 5–10 minutes per import session and appreciate the side effect: actively reviewing every transaction before it enters your budget improves awareness of where money is going.
