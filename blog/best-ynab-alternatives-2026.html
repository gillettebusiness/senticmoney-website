Why Are People Switching from YNAB?

YNAB (You Need A Budget) is one of the most effective budgeting apps available, but its $180/year price tag and steep learning curve push many users to seek alternatives.

Common reasons people leave YNAB:

  • Price increases: YNAB was $50/year in 2014, $84 in 2017, $99 in 2021, and jumped to $180 in 2024—a 260% increase that sparked significant community backlash
  • Learning curve: YNAB's "four rules" methodology takes weeks to master, and many users never fully adapt
  • Zero-based budgeting isn't for everyone: Some users prefer simpler tracking without allocating every dollar
  • Plaid concerns: YNAB uses Plaid for bank connections, which some users find uncomfortable
  • Mobile-first users: YNAB's mobile app exists but works best with desktop—some prefer mobile-native apps

That said, YNAB works brilliantly for people who embrace its methodology. If YNAB's system clicked for you but the price doesn't, this guide will help you find cheaper alternatives that support similar approaches.

Budgeting Styles Explained

Zero-Based Budgeting

Every dollar of income is assigned to a category before you spend. Your budget "balances to zero." YNAB, EveryDollar, and Actual use this approach.

Tracking-Based Budgeting

Set spending limits per category and track against them. Less strict than zero-based—you're monitoring rather than pre-allocating. Monarch and Simplifi use this approach.

Envelope Budgeting

Divide money into virtual "envelopes" for each spending category. When an envelope is empty, you stop spending. Goodbudget focuses on this method.

If you love YNAB's zero-based methodology and want to keep using it, several alternatives support the same approach—some at lower cost or for free.

  2. Embrace Your True Expenses — Budget for irregular expenses monthly

What About Privacy-Focused Alternatives?

YNAB stores your financial data in the cloud and uses Plaid for bank connections. If these practices concern you, privacy-focused alternatives exist.

The Plaid Issue

Plaid is a financial data aggregator used by YNAB, Monarch, Simplifi, and most budgeting apps. When you connect a bank account, Plaid stores your credentials and transaction data on their servers.

This means:

  • A third party has access to your bank login credentials
  • Your transaction history exists on external servers
  • Data breaches at Plaid could expose your financial information

Can I Migrate My YNAB Data?

YNAB allows data export, so you can move your transaction history and budget to a new app.

Exporting from YNAB:

  1. Log into YNAB web app
  2. Click your budget name → Settings
  3. Scroll to "Export Budget"
  4. Download the CSV file

This exports transactions with dates, payees, categories, and amounts.

What Imports Well:

  • Transaction history: Most apps can import CSV transactions
  • Account balances: You may need to set these manually

What Doesn't Migrate:

  • Budget targets and goals: You'll need to recreate these
  • Category structure: Each app has different category systems
  • Scheduled transactions: Usually require re-entry

Importing to SenticMoney:

  1. Export your YNAB transactions as CSV
  2. Open SenticMoney → Transactions → Import
  3. Select the CSV file
  4. Map columns (date, payee, amount, category)
  5. Review and import

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is YNAB so expensive?

YNAB costs $180/year ($15/month) because it includes automatic bank syncing via Plaid, cloud storage, cross-device sync, and ongoing development. YNAB raised prices from $50 to $84 to $99 and now $180 over the years, frustrating many long-time users.

Is there a free version of YNAB?

YNAB offers a 34-day free trial but no permanent free tier. After the trial, you must pay $180/year to continue. Alternatives like SenticMoney and EveryDollar offer free versions with core budgeting features.

Is Monarch Money better than YNAB?

Monarch Money ($144/year) is better if you want Mint-style tracking with automatic categorization. YNAB is better if you want strict zero-based budgeting methodology. Monarch is easier to learn; YNAB has a steeper learning curve but more budgeting discipline.

Can I do zero-based budgeting without YNAB?

Yes. EveryDollar was designed specifically for zero-based budgeting by Dave Ramsey. SenticMoney also supports zero-based principles—you can allocate income across categories until your "to budget" amount reaches zero.

Does YNAB use Plaid?

Yes. YNAB uses Plaid for automatic bank connections. If you're concerned about Plaid security, you can use YNAB in manual mode, or switch to an app like SenticMoney that doesn't use Plaid at all.
