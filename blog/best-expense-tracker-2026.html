What Makes a Good Expense Tracker in 2026?

A good expense tracker in 2026 needs accurate categorization, flexible import options, clear spending summaries, and strong data protection. According to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, tracking every dollar you spend is the foundation of financial health, but the tool you choose should not put your financial data at risk in the process.

The expense tracker landscape has shifted significantly since Mint shut down in January 2024. Millions of users were forced to find alternatives, and many discovered that the apps they switched to required handing over bank login credentials through Plaid, a third-party data aggregator. In 2026, the best expense trackers give you a choice about how much data you share.

Here is what to evaluate when choosing an expense tracking app:

  • Categorization: Can you create custom categories, subcategories, and tags? Does the app auto-categorize transactions?
  • Import flexibility: Does it support CSV, Excel, OFX, QFX, and PDF files, or only Plaid bank linking?
  • Reporting: Can you see monthly summaries, spending trends, and breakdowns by category?
  • Data storage: Is your data stored in the cloud (on company servers) or locally on your device?
  • Cost: What do you actually get for free versus what requires a paid subscription?
  • Receipt tracking: Can you scan and attach receipts to transactions?

2. YNAB — Best for Zero-Based Budget Tracking

Price: $109/year (annual) or $14.99/month

Platform: Web, iOS, Android

YNAB (You Need A Budget) is widely regarded as the gold standard for zero-based budgeting, where every dollar gets assigned a job. Its expense tracking is tightly integrated with its budgeting philosophy: when you spend money, it immediately shows how that affects your budget categories.

YNAB connects to banks through Plaid for automatic transaction imports. It offers robust categorization, goal tracking, and detailed spending reports. The learning curve is steeper than most apps, but users who commit to the method often report significant financial improvements.

Expense tracking strengths: Automatic bank import, real-time budget impact, goal tracking, category-based reporting, multi-platform sync.

Limitations: Most expensive option at $109/year. No free tier. Requires Plaid for bank sync. Cloud-only storage. If you want similar features for less, check out our budgeting software comparison.

Can You Track Expenses Without Linking Your Bank?

Yes, you can track expenses effectively without ever linking your bank account through Plaid. Manual entry, CSV imports, and receipt scanning all provide accurate expense tracking without sharing your banking credentials with a third party. According to Investopedia, Plaid connects to over 12,000 financial institutions by storing user credentials, which is a trade-off many privacy-conscious users prefer to avoid.

Three approaches let you track expenses without Plaid:

  1. Manual entry: Log transactions as you make them. SenticMoney, EveryDollar (free), and GoodBudget all support this. It takes 30 seconds per transaction and keeps you highly aware of your spending.
  2. Bank statement imports: Download your statement as a CSV, Excel, OFX, QFX, or PDF file from your bank's website, then import it into SenticMoney. This gives you the same transaction data without sharing your login. SenticMoney includes 15+ bank presets for automatic column mapping.
  3. Receipt scanning: SenticMoney's Standard tier lets you photograph receipts and extract transaction details using AI Vision, or process email receipts (.eml files) locally on your device.

The bank statement import method is particularly powerful because you get the same data that Plaid would pull, but you control the process. You log into your bank directly, download the file, and import it on your terms. No third party ever sees your credentials.
